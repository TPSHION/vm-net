vm-net

Privacy Policy

This page explains what data vm-net handles, why it is handled, and which parts stay on your device versus being sent to external services.

Last updated: April 17, 2026

1. Summary

  • vm-net does not require account registration.
  • vm-net primarily stores preferences and recent results locally on your Mac.
  • vm-net does not include third-party advertising SDKs or analytics SDKs in the current project.
  • When you run network features, the app sends requests to the relevant external services needed to complete those features.
  • When you buy or restore the Desktop Pet unlock, Apple processes the purchase through StoreKit.

2. Data Stored Locally on Your Device

vm-net stores app data locally using Apple system storage such as UserDefaults. Based on the current project, local data may include:

  • App language and display preferences
  • Floating capsule visibility, colors, transparency, and last position
  • Desktop Pet settings, selected pet, and roaming behavior
  • Recent speed test results
  • Recent network diagnosis results and selected diagnosis target
  • Desktop Pet trial start time and local unlock state cache

This local data is used to preserve your settings and recent history between app launches.

3. Real-Time Network Monitoring

vm-net monitors current network throughput on your Mac so it can show upload and download speed in the menu bar or floating capsule.

This monitoring happens locally on your device. In the current project, it is used to display network speed and does not require you to create an account.

4. Speed Test

If you choose to run a speed test, vm-net connects to Measurement Lab (M-Lab) services to locate a test server and perform download and upload measurements.

  • The app sends requests required to locate and communicate with an M-Lab test server.
  • The resulting speed test data may be processed by M-Lab.
  • The current app copy explicitly warns that test data may be shared publicly through M-Lab.

If you do not want this behavior, do not start a speed test.

5. Network Diagnosis

If you run network diagnosis, vm-net performs connectivity checks for a selected target host. Based on the current project, this includes:

  • Checking local network path status
  • Resolving the selected host through DNS
  • Sending an HTTPS request to the selected target

These checks are used only to show diagnosis results inside the app. As with any network request, the destination service and the network infrastructure involved may receive your IP address and standard request metadata needed to complete the connection.

6. App Store Update Check

If you use the in-app update check, vm-net sends your app bundle identifier to Apple's App Store lookup service to compare the current installed version with the latest App Store version.

If a newer version is available, the app opens the App Store page for vm-net.

7. In-App Purchase for Desktop Pet

vm-net offers an optional one-time unlock for the Desktop Pet feature. Purchases and restore actions are handled through Apple's StoreKit and App Store infrastructure.

  • Apple may process transaction data, account status, and entitlement data needed to complete or restore the purchase.
  • vm-net stores local state needed to track the trial period and reflect the unlock state inside the app.
  • vm-net checks current entitlements through Apple to determine whether the feature is unlocked.

8. Third-Party Services

Based on the current project, vm-net may interact with the following external services when you use specific features:

  • Measurement Lab (M-Lab) for speed testing
  • Selected diagnosis target hosts for DNS and HTTPS connectivity checks
  • Apple App Store services for update lookup
  • Apple StoreKit / App Store services for purchases and restore actions

Those services operate under their own privacy terms and policies.

9. Data Retention and Control

Local settings and recent results remain on your device until you change them, reset the app's data, or remove the app and its container data.

If you want to clear locally stored app data, you can remove the app's stored preferences and container data from your Mac. Purchase records handled by Apple are governed by Apple's systems and policies.

10. Children's Privacy

vm-net is a general utility app and is not designed specifically for children.

11. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated when vm-net changes how it handles data or adds new features. The latest version will be published on this page.
